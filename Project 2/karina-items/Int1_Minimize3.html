Ever heard of Sigmund Freud?

Introducing the ID. The ID in an individual represents their instinct. It’s what comes first, the primitive and instinctive component of a personality. It presents itself in the unconscious mind, as the impulsive aspect. For instance, a baby’s personality and actions all comes from the id and unconsicous mind. It remains the same throughout your life, and does not change with experience. Introducing the EGO. The EGO is the part of the id that has been modified or influenced directly by the external world. It serves as a mediator between the id and the external world. Its purpose is to be the decision-maker aspect of your personality. If we were to compare it with the id, the ego would make decisions based of off reason, whereas the id is chaotic and unreasonable. Indroducing the SUPEREGO. The superego is what the individual learns from it’s primary surroundings, such as parents and other close individuals. This phase occurs between the ages of 3-5. It controls the id’s impulses (often forbidden ones such as sex and aggression). It aids the ego in turning into moralistic goals rather than realistic, and strives for perfections.
